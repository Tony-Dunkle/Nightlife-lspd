Departmental Policies and Guidelines

1.0 Administrative Policies ▸

1.1 Mission and Commitment

Los Santos Police Department (LSPD) officers are officially certified law enforcement personnel within the state of San Andreas. They are vested with the Authority to enforce the Constitution of the United States, as well as all applicable state and local laws within the designated county limits. Their primary responsibility is to maintain public safety order and preserve peace within the community.

1.1.2 Code of Conduct

All LSPD officers are mandated to uphold the most stringent standards of professionalism, integrity, and ethical conduct at all times, both on and off duty. The actions and behavior of individual officers directly reflect upon the entire department and its reputation. Any instance of misconduct, whether internal or external, will be subject to a thorough investigation and may result in a range of disciplinary actions, up to and including termination of employment or referral to criminal authorities. Strict accountability shall be applied to all actions undertaken by department personnel.

1.1.3 Code of Ethics

Officers of the Los Santos Police Department shall adhere to the following ethical principles: Service and Protection, Constitutional Adherence, Exemplary Leadership, Self-Control and Impartiality, Confidentiality, Anti-Corruption, Continuous Improvement. The LSPD badge symbolizes the public's trust and confidence in the organization. Officers are honor-bound to uphold this trust through diligent adherence to ethical conduct and full accountability for their actions.

1.1.4 Oath of Honor

The Oath of Honor serves as a solemn declaration guiding the behavior and principles of every LSPD officer: "On my honor, I will never betray my integrity, my character, or the public trust. I will have the courage to hold myself and others accountable. I will maintain the highest ethical standards and uphold the values of my community and department." Core Values: Honor, Integrity, Accountability, Courage, Public Trust.

1.2 Organization ▸

1.2.1 Chain of Command

The Los Santos Police Department operates under a strict Chain of Command established to ensure clear lines of communication, effective leadership, and comprehensive accountability across all operational levels. This includes High Command, Command Staff, Supervisors, and Patrol Officers. Officers report to their supervisors, and bypassing ranks is generally prohibited. Adherence to the Chain of Command is paramount, and failure to follow established channels may result in disciplinary action.

1.2.2 Ranks and Roles

The LSPD rank structure defines specific roles, responsibilities, and authorities: Chief of Police, Deputy Chief, Captain, Lieutenant, Master Sergeant, Staff Sergeant, Sergeant, Corporal, Senior Officer, Officer, and Cadet. Each rank has specific duties, disciplinary authority, and identification.

1.2.3 Divisions & Units

The LSPD is structured into specialized divisions and units including: Patrol Division (with Field Training Officers - FTO), H.E.A.T. (Highway Enforcement of Aggressive Traffic), MOTO (Motorcycle Unit), A.S.U. (Air Support Unit), Combat Medic, Narcotics Unit, K9 Unit, S.W.A.T. (Special Weapons and Tactics), and Internal Affairs (IA).

1.2.4 Uniform Policy

All officers must wear department-approved uniforms while on duty. Unauthorized civilian attire or insignia is strictly prohibited and may result in disciplinary action. The wearing of LSPD uniforms or insignia while off-duty is forbidden unless explicitly authorized.

1.3 Hiring Process ▸

1.3.1 Eligibility Requirements

Prospective applicants must have no prior felony convictions and possess a valid San Andreas driver's license.

1.3.2 Application Steps

The application process involves formal application submission, a thorough background check, and an interview invitation for qualified applicants.

1.3.3 Post-Interview Protocol

Successful candidates enter a probationary Cadet phase. Cadets must contact an assigned FTO (Sergeant rank or higher) within 14 days of acceptance. After FTO interview and structured training, they enter a "Solo Cadet" phase for a minimum of 14 days before Officer promotion eligibility. Exceptions require Command Staff approval.

1.4 Disciplinary Actions ▸

1.4.1 Strike System

LSPD officers are subject to a three-strike disciplinary system, with each strike expiring after 30 days. Cadets are terminated after a second strike. Penalties are: 1st Strike (3-day suspension from divisional assignments), 2nd Strike (demotion, 7-day suspension), 3rd Strike (immediate termination and permanent reapplication ban). Violations are reported to Internal Affairs.

1.4.2 Suspension Policy

Lieutenants or higher can suspend officers with one active strike for 3 days. Excessive force instances lead to immediate suspension pending investigation. Suspended officers are prohibited from on-duty activities, must return gear, cannot present as law enforcement, and must maintain 25 feet distance from LSPD property unless instructed otherwise. Suspension requests from Command Staff require documentation and High Command approval.

1.4.3 Demotion

Captains or higher can demote officers with two active strikes. Probationary officers may be terminated or reassigned with Command Staff approval.

1.4.4 Termination

Captains or higher can terminate officers for three active strikes or as a result of an administrative hearing. Terminated officers must surrender credentials and gear and are removed from rosters. Appeals can be filed with the Deputy Chief. Rehired officers are reinstated one rank lower than their previous rank.

1.4.5 Strike Removal

Officers can appeal strike removal after 14 days for hourly violations (with demonstrated activity adherence) or 7 days for misconduct (with demonstrated conduct improvement). 50% of the strike's weight may be removed initially, with full removal after 30 clean disciplinary days. Appeals are not accepted during LOA.

1.5 Workplace Conduct & Harassment Policy ▸

1.5.1 Respect & Equality

All LSPD employees must contribute to a respectful and professional workplace. Harassment, bullying, and discrimination (based on age, gender, race, etc.) are strictly prohibited and will be investigated and disciplined.

1.5.2 Interpersonal Conduct

Personnel must maintain professional relationships with colleagues and allied agencies. Disruptive or unprofessional behavior is not tolerated and may result in counseling, disciplinary points, or more severe actions for repeat offenses.

1.6 Callsigns ▸

Each officer has a unique callsign linked to their rank/unit. Consistent use of the callsign is mandatory in all official communications (radio, inter-agency, reports). Example: Callsign 245 | J. Doe.

1.7 Activity Requirements ▸

Minimum weekly activity hours: Officer (8 hrs), Sergeant/Lieutenant (10 hrs), Captain (12 hrs including administrative tasks).

1.7.1 Leave of Absence (LOA) Policy

All LOA requests must be pre-approved by Command Staff. Misuse of LOA is subject to disciplinary action.

1.7.2 Clock-In/Clock-Out Policy

All employees must accurately clock in/out for shifts. First offense for missed clock-in/out results in verbal warning and manual adjustment; repeat offenses may nullify recorded time. Errors are reported to High Command. Three warnings max before strike points are issued.

1.7.3 Sign-In / Sign-Out

All personnel must formally sign on/off duty via the Mobile Data Terminal (MDT) at the beginning and end of each patrol/shift. Officers must sign off when engaging in non-LSPD tasks or unable to respond to calls. Failure to comply leads to disciplinary action.

1.7.4 Weekly Hour Requirements

Cadet / Officer / Deputy: 8 hours; Sergeant / Lieutenant: 10 hours; Captain+: 12 hours (including administrative time).

1.7.5 Leave of Absence (LOA)

Officers unable to meet hour requirements must submit formal LOA requests in advance, approved through the chain of command. LOAs cannot be retroactive, must have justifiable reasons, and are not for evading work. Officers on approved LOA cannot conduct training or issue certifications.

1.7.6 Extended LOA (ELOA)

Leave exceeding 14 days requires an ELOA request through the Division Head, with a maximum duration of 8 weeks. Officers on ELOA must check in with their supervisor every two weeks; failure to contact for 30 consecutive days may result in termination. Return from ELOA may necessitate rank review or reapplication.

1.8 Certifications ▸

Certifications are formal qualifications from departmental training. Officers on LOA/ELOA cannot conduct training, issue certifications, or serve in FTO/specialized roles.

1.9 Resignations ▸

Employees can resign at any time. Rehire does not guarantee former rank. All department-issued equipment and credentials must be surrendered to a Supervisor upon resignation.

1.10 Tattoo Policy ▸

Facial tattoos are prohibited. Visible tattoos must not be disrespectful, demeaning, derogatory, or discriminatory. Violations may lead to corrective action and disciplinary measures.

1.11 Duty to Intercede & Report ▸

Officers must intercede and report any excessive force by law enforcement personnel. Required actions: intervention (if safe), reporting to a Supervisor as soon as possible, and filing a comprehensive Use of Force Statement.

1.12 Chain of Command & Orders ▸

1.12.1 Unity of Command

Each officer reports to only one direct supervisor per task, with exceptions for urgent operational needs from higher-ranking supervisors.

1.12.2 Unlawful Orders

Officers are strictly prohibited from following unlawful or policy-violating orders. If uncertain, officers must seek clarification or escalate to higher authority. Officers are responsible for justifying refusal.

1.12.3 Conflicting Orders

If lawful orders conflict, inform the supervisor who issued the latter order, who is responsible for resolution. Following the clarified order will not incur liability for disregarding the initial conflicting order.

2.0 Operational Procedures ▸

2.1 Robberies & Hostage Situations

2.1.1 General Protocol

Life preservation is paramount. Officers can honor reasonable demands for hostage safety. Harm to hostages due to poor negotiation may lead to disciplinary action. SWAT typically manages high-risk scenes; otherwise, the highest-ranking officer commands. Suspect requests (e.g., "no AIR1") must be adhered to. After departure, debrief hostages, inspect scene, secure area, and file report.

2.1.2 Hostage Negotiation Basics

Key principles: Proof of Life, avoid direct refusal (explain limitations), and Five Negotiation Steps (Active Listening, Empathy, Rapport, Influence, Behavioral Change). Use affirmations, paraphrasing, mirroring, and labeling emotions.

2.1.3 Robbery Negotiation Checklist

Preparation (backup, log license plates), Hostage Safety (prioritize, get names), Suspect Engagement (log demands, tactfully decline unreasonable, consider SWAT), Pursuit Preparation (assign units, caution with spike strips), Rescue Protocol.

2.1.4 House Robberies

Response limited to 4 units. Use flashlights for evidence. Apprehended suspects' fingerprints matched at PD. Contact Detectives for interrogations.

2.1.5 Suspicious Activity

Response limited to 2 units. Pursuit authorized if suspect flees. Additional units only upon escalation.

2.1.6 Car Boosting

Response limited to 4 units. Additional units only if suspects fire shots.

2.1.7 Gang Shootouts

At Compounds: Establish perimeter, wait 5 min after last gunshot before entering. EMS at safe distance until Code 4. In Public: Prioritize de-escalation, move conflict away from civilians. Issue warning before "Code Red" (Lethal Force Authorized). All survivors subject to GSR test.

2.2 Vehicle Pursuits

2.2.1 General Pursuit Guidelines

Start with clear radio transmission (vehicle info, direction, reason, traffic). Max 4 ground units + AIR1/HEAT. Unit 4 runs parallel for observation/support. PIT maneuver authorized after 5 min or Supervisor approval (prohibited >100 MPH or striking front bumper). 30-second interval between PITs. Prohibited on damaged vehicles.

2.2.2 Disabling Tires

Sidearms to disable tires authorized only under severe public safety risk: suspects firing, repeatedly ramming, or intentionally striking pedestrians/officers. Last resort.

2.2.3 Vehicle Threat Codes

Code Blue (Tire Disabling Authorized): 3 confirmed rams, 2+ NVL jumps, pursuit >10 min, or escalating public safety threat. 20-sec cool-down between shots. Code Red (Lethal Force Authorized): Declared by highest-ranking officer. Authorized if final warning ignored, suspects fire at officers, or emergency/police/military vehicle stolen.

2.3 Use of Force Levels

Minimum force necessary. Progressive continuum: Level 1 (Officer Presence), Level 2 (Verbal Commands), Level 3 (Physical Control - tackling, restraining), Level 4 (Less-Lethal Force - Batons, Tasers up to 3 times after 2 failed hand-to-hand attempts, 20-sec cool-down), Level 5 (Lethal Force - sidearms, SMGs, rifles, for immediate credible threat of death/serious bodily harm, requires intent, capability, immediate threat). After Use of Force: Medical Transport, Arrest Decision (after medical clearance), Lethal Force Notification to supervisor.

2.3.1 Apprehensions

Initial restraint with handcuffs. Miranda Rights read immediately after cuffing and before questioning. Downed suspects (after shots fired): GSR test, Terry frisk if positive, full search if weapons found. Move suspect to safe location for medical aid. Shackles for flight risk. Treat suspects professionally, use de-escalation.

2.3.2 Downed Officer Protocol

Secure and recover downed personnel ASAP. If looted, officer authorized to revive until safely retrieved, transported, and department-issued gear fully inventoried/secured.

2.3.2.1 Panic Button Use

Only for critical, life-threatening emergencies (officer shot/incapacitated, immediate comprehensive departmental response). Prohibited for non-critical situations, off-duty, or when standard communication suffices. Misuse results in disciplinary action.

2.3.3 Matched firepower

Officers match suspect force with minimum necessary. Class 1/2 weapons (pistols, shotguns, SMGs, illegal mods): respond with Class 1 sidearm. Class 2 SMGs/Rifles or Class 3 weapons (fully auto/high-powered rifles): respond with Class 3 weapons. Firepower should not escalate beyond neutralizing immediate threat.

2.4 Radio Usage

All transmissions start with officer's sign. Messages brief, precise, professional. Remain on primary dispatch channel unless reassigned. Prioritized traffic: pursuits, confirmed shots fired, robbery in progress, officer in danger. Wait for pauses. Idle conversation reserved for non-radio means.

2.4.1 Ten-Codes (10-Codes)

Standardized radio codes for rapid/clear communication. Required use in all transmissions. (Includes a detailed table of 10-Codes and their meanings).

2.5 Reasonable Suspicion vs. Probable Cause

2.5.1 Reasonable Suspicion

Legal standard based on articulable facts. Allows officers to: Stop and Question, Conduct Terry Frisks, Administer GSR/sobriety tests.

2.5.2 Probable Cause

Higher legal standard, requires factual evidence of crime. Necessary for: Searches, Seizures, Arrests. Example: marijuana odor (reasonable suspicion) + blunt in plain view/slurred speech (probable cause).

2.5.3 Detainment

Temporary detention based on reasonable suspicion, not arrest. Terry frisk permitted if armed/contraband suspicion. Release promptly if no probable cause. Transport to station permissible if not escalating to unlawful arrest.

2.6 Terry Frisk

Protective pat-down of outer clothing during lawful detention, based on reasonable suspicion of weapons/contraband. Procedure: use departmental commands (/me pats down), then action command (/e). Permissible findings: weapons, large drug quantities (felony threshold). Prohibited: seizing unrelated items unless probable cause for full search/arrest is established.

2.7 Miranda Rights

Must be read verbatim to suspects in custody before questioning. Failure makes statements inadmissible. Required only when in custody AND being interrogated. Does not necessitate automatic release. Document all instances.

2.8 Vehicle Impounds

Authorized for vehicles in criminal activity/significant traffic violations. $2,000 release fee. Durations vary: Illegal Parking (2 hrs), 10-80 Pursuit (6 hrs), 10-80 with Gunfire (12 hrs), Interfering with Law Enforcement (6 hrs), Heist/Cartel/Bank Involvement (12 hrs), Drug Distribution (4 hrs), Excessive Speeding (2 hrs). All impounds logged in MDT and justified in report.

2.9 Traffic Enforcement

2.9.1 Speed Limits

City: 50 MPH. Highways/Freeways: 80 MPH. Exemptions for Code 2/3 responses, but with due regard for public safety.

2.9.2 Red Lights

Treat as four-way stops during patrol. Code 2: complete stop, short siren chirp, proceed with caution. Code 3: proceed with caution, prioritize clearing intersection. Collision Protocol: stop, assess, render assistance.

2.9.3 Traffic Stops

Notify dispatch with vehicle/occupant details. Safe, methodical approach. Explain reason, request ID. Outcomes: Verbal warning, Written warning, Citation, Arrest (if probable cause).

2.9.4 Felony (Code 5) Stops

For serious crimes/felony evasion. Immediate backup mandatory. Loud, clear commands from cover. Detain occupants safely, systematically, with firearms drawn until secure/compliant.

2.9.5 Verbal Warnings

Log minor infraction warnings in MDT: DD-MM-YYYY: <#> demerit points for issued by .

2.9.6 Demerit Points

2 demerit points per traffic violation. License revoked at 10+ points (via MDT). Incident report required for citations/infractions with demerit points/revocation.

2.10 Weapon Usage

2.10.1 Authorized Weapons by Use

Baton (defensive, compliance). Taser (non-compliant, actively resisting/threatening, physical control insufficient). Handgun (credible threat of serious bodily harm/death). Assault Rifle (Class 3) (armed/high-risk suspects, requires appropriate rank approval).

2.11 Vehicle Usage

Operate only authorized LSPD vehicles. Cadets must ride with FTO or Corporal+.

2.12 Equipment, Cameras, and Off-Duty Protocol

2.12.1 Body & Dash Cameras

Body Cameras: Active and worn on duty, footage retained 72 hrs. Dash Cameras: Footage admissible only if correctly installed/operational. Panic Buttons: Functional and accessible on duty.

2.12.2 Personal Body Cameras

Only visible/identifiable (e.g., GoPro, Axon) admissible. Department vests presumed to have integrated cameras.

2.12.3 Institutional Surveillance

Footage from PDs/hospitals presumed recorded and admissible.

2.12.4 Reporting Window for Footage Requests

Civilians: 24-hour window. Agencies: Respond to requests within 24 hours.

2.12.5 Off-Duty Equipment

Carry: Body armor (worn only if immediate threat), handcuffs, radio. Securely store: Class 3 weapons, taser, baton, flashlight, IFAKs, spike strips, body armor (if not worn).

2.12.6 Off-Duty Conduct

Clock out (MDT, 10-42). Maintain high professional conduct/discretion. Prohibited from knowingly associating with convicted felons/gang members (except immediate family).

2.13 Scene Control

Manage incident scenes/crowds for public safety. Progression: Verbal Requests, Lawful Orders, Detainment (if non-compliant). Bystanders: maintain minimum 24 feet. Recording by civilians permitted if not interfering/compromising scene integrity.

2.14 Report Writing

All official reports comprehensive, addressing Who, What, Where, When, Why, How. Required Inclusions: Visuals (mugshot/inventory photos), Personnel (officer names), Charges & Plea (suspect charges/plea status), Evidence (linking suspect to crime).
